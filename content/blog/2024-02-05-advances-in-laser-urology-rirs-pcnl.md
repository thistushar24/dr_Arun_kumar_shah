## What is Laser Urology?

Laser urology utilizes high-intensity, precisely calibrated medical lasers—most notably the **Holmium:YAG laser** and **Thulium Fiber Laser (TFL)**—transmitted through ultra-thin, flexible optical fibers. These fibers are passed through natural urinary openings (endoscopy) or tiny keyhole punctures directly to the target tissue or kidney stone.

Because laser energy is absorbed within a fraction of a millimeter in water, it vaporizes stones and seals blood vessels instantly with zero damage to surrounding healthy organs.

## Key Laser Procedures Offered at National Urology Center

### 1. Retrograde Intrarenal Surgery (RIRS) for Kidney Stones
RIRS represents the pinnacle of stone management technology. A highly flexible ureteroscope (thinner than a pencil) is introduced through the urethra and bladder up into the intricate collecting system of the kidney.
- **How it works:** Once the stone is visualized on a high-definition monitor, a Holmium laser fiber fires rapid pulses of optical energy, shattering the stone into fine, dust-like particles that wash out effortlessly.
- **Why patients love it:** Complete absence of surgical cuts or scars on the skin, virtually zero blood loss, and discharge within 24 hours.

### 2. Mini-PCNL (Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy) for Large Stones
When a kidney stone is too massive (such as staghorn stones over 2 to 3 cm) for RIRS, Mini-PCNL is utilized.
- **How it works:** Rather than a 15-cm open surgical cut, a miniature keyhole tract (less than half an inch) is established directly into the kidney under X-ray and ultrasound guidance. Miniaturized nephroscopes and laser lithotripters break and aspirate the stone fragments safely.

### 3. HoLEP (Holmium Laser Enucleation of the Prostate) for BPH
For men suffering from severe urinary obstruction due to benign prostate enlargement, HoLEP is the gold-standard surgical treatment globally.
- **How it works:** The laser fiber peels away the entire overgrown adenoma lobe of the prostate—much like peeling the segments of an orange—pushing it into the bladder where a specialized morcellator instrument vacuums it out.
- **Why it’s revolutionary:** Extremely low re-treatment rate, immediate restoration of strong urinary flow, and safe execution even in elderly patients with heart pacemakers or those taking anticoagulant blood thinners.
